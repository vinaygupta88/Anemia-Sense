About

Anemia is a condition with low red blood cells or hemoglobin, causing fatigue, weakness, shortness of breath, and pale skin. Causes include nutritional deficiencies, diseases, and blood loss. Treatments vary accordingly.

Hemoglobin

Hemoglobin, a protein in red blood cells, transports oxygen and carbon dioxide. Healthy ranges: Men 13.2–16.6 g/dL, Women 11.6–15 g/dL.

MCH

MCH refers to the average hemoglobin per red blood cell. Normal range: 27.5–33.2 pg.

MCHC

MCHC is the average hemoglobin concentration in RBCs. Normal range: 33.4–35.5 g/dL.

MCV

MCV measures average red blood cell size. Normal range: 80–100 fL.
